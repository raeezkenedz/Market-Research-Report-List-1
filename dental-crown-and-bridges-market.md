<p><p>The dental crown and bridge market consists of various materials, each offering distinct benefits. Ceramic crowns provide a natural appearance, ideal for front teeth. Porcelain crowns combine aesthetics and strength but may be less durable. Gold crowns, known for their longevity and biocompatibility, are often used for molars. Titanium crowns are lightweight and resistant to corrosion, suitable for implants. Metal alloy crowns offer strength and cost-effectiveness, while acrylic crowns are typically used for temporary restorations due to their affordability.</p></p>
