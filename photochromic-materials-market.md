<p><p>Photochromic materials are classified into different types based on their properties and applications. T-type (thermally reversible) photochromics change color in response to light and revert to their original state with a temperature change. P-type (photo-induced) materials undergo a permanent color change when exposed to light. Other types include those mixed or hybrid materials that exhibit both T-type and P-type characteristics, expanding their utility in various applications like eyewear, coatings, and sensors. The market continues to grow due to increasing demand for innovative products.</p></p>
