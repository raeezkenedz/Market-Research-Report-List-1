<p><ul><li>Subunit vaccines</li><li>Live attenuated vaccines</li><li>Conjugate vaccines</li><li>Inactivated vaccines</li><li>Recombinant vector vaccines</li><li>Toxoid vaccines</li><li>Synthetic vaccines</li><li>Dendritic cell vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vaccines and vaccination market encompasses various types, each with distinct mechanisms. Subunit vaccines utilize specific protein subunits to elicit an immune response. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened pathogens, triggering strong immunity. Conjugate vaccines link polysaccharides to proteins to enhance response, while inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens. Recombinant vector vaccines employ harmless viruses to deliver genetic material. Toxoid vaccines target toxins produced by pathogens, and synthetic vaccines are designed using computer-aided technology. Dendritic cell vaccines harness immune cells to initiate targeted responses.</p></p>
