<p><p>Rotational anemometers are devices used to measure wind speed and direction, commonly utilized in meteorology and environmental studies. The four-cup anemometer features four cups mounted on arms that rotate in the wind, providing accurate measurements due to its stability and sensitivity. In contrast, the three-cup anemometer, which has one less cup, is more compact and commonly used for simpler applications. Both types are essential for assessing atmospheric conditions, with the choice depending on measurement requirements and environmental factors.</p></p>
