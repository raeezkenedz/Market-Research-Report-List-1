<p><p>Carbon fiber is a lightweight, high-strength material used in the automobile market, primarily in three forms: Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer (CFRP), Ceramic Matrix Composites (CMC), and Metal Matrix Composites (MMC). CFRP combines carbon fiber with polymers to create strong, durable components suitable for various auto parts. CMC uses carbon fibers within ceramic matrices to enhance thermal resistance and toughness, ideal for high-performance applications. MMC integrates carbon fibers with metals, improving strength and reducing weight, benefiting performance-oriented vehicles.</p></p>
