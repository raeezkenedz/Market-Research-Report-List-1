<p><p>The smoked haddock market is primarily divided into two types: hot-smoked and cold-smoked. Hot-smoked haddock is processed at high temperatures, resulting in a flaky texture and a rich, intense flavor, making it ideal for ready-to-eat meals and cooking. In contrast, cold-smoked haddock undergoes a lower temperature process, preserving its delicate texture and subtle flavor, often used in gourmet dishes or salads. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, with varying culinary applications and market demand.</p></p>
