<p><p>Laboratory-grown diamonds (LGDs) are synthetic diamonds created in controlled environments, mimicking natural processes. There are two primary methods for their production: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT). CVD grows diamonds from carbon gas, allowing for precise control over quality and characteristics. In contrast, HPHT simulates the extreme conditions under which natural diamonds form. Both methods cater to various market segments, including jewelry, industrial applications, and investment opportunities, offering a sustainable alternative to mined diamonds.</p></p>
