<p><p>The high-end FPGA market comprises three key types: SRAM, Flash, and Antifuse. SRAM FPGAs are popular for their reprogrammability and high performance, making them suitable for complex applications. Flash FPGAs offer non-volatile storage, ideal for designs needing resilience against power loss. Antifuse FPGAs, though less common, provide permanent configurations with enhanced security features, making them appealing for sensitive applications. Together, these technologies cater to diverse needs in industries like telecommunications, automotive, and aerospace, driving innovation.</p></p>
