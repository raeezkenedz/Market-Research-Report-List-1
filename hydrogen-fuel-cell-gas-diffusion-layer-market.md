<p><p>The Hydrogen Fuel Cell Gas Diffusion Layer (GDL) is a critical component in proton exchange membrane fuel cells (PEMFCs). It facilitates the distribution of reactant gases (hydrogen and oxygen) to the catalyst layer while managing water and heat within the fuel cell. The GDL is typically made of porous carbon materials, such as carbon paper or carbon cloth, which ensure efficient gas diffusion, electrical conductivity, and mechanical stability. Its role is vital in optimizing fuel cell performance, durability, and efficiency.</p><p>The Hydrogen Fuel Cell Gas Diffusion Layer Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing adoption of hydrogen fuel cells across various industries, including automotive, aerospace, and stationary power generation.
