<p><p>The cosmetic fragrance market is divided into two primary types: pure natural extraction and chemical extraction. Pure natural extraction involves sourcing fragrances directly from plants and flowers through methods like steam distillation or cold pressing, offering a more organic appeal. In contrast, chemical extraction uses synthetic processes to create fragrances, which can replicate complex scents and are often more cost-effective. While natural extracts cater to consumers seeking eco-friendly options, chemical fragrances are popular for their consistency and variety.</p></p>
